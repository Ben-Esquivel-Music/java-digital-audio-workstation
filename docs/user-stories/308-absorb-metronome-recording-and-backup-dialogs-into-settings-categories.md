# Absorb the Metronome (Recording) and Backup Dialogs into Settings Categories

## Motivation

After story 307 folds the audio device dialog into the Audio category, two of the four scattered surfaces from the Settings View Design Book ┬¦1.1 remain as standalone modals: `MetronomeSettingsDialog` (click side-output channel, gain, main-mix, cue bus) and `BackupSettingsDialog` (retention sliders + a disk-usage pie). Stage 4 (┬¦7) folds the metronome dialog into a Recording category and the backup dialog ŌĆö pie chart and all ŌĆö into a Backups category of the Rail & Pane shell, and turns their standalone entry points into deep links.

This finishes the book's ┬¦1.1 / ┬¦2.1 "one front door" goal:

- **┬¦1.1 / ┬¦2.1 ŌĆö four windows become one navigated surface.** With audio absorbed (307) and Recording + Backups absorbed here, all four of the book's ┬¦1.1 dialogs are categories of a single surface. A user who wants "a setting" navigates one rail; they never have to predict which of four windows holds it.
- **┬¦1.10 ŌĆö keep the rich domain views, fold them in.** The backup disk-usage pie and the metronome routing controls are genuinely useful (┬¦1.10 "fold in, not throw away"). The pie becomes a *group-level visual* inside the Backups category (┬¦5.8), not a separate dialog; the metronome routing becomes Recording-category rows (┬¦3.3: Metronome ┬Ę Click routing ┬Ę Cue mix).
- **┬¦2.7 / ┬¦6.6 ŌĆö disk-usage off the FX thread.** Computing backup disk usage is I/O. `BackupSettingsDialog` already does this off-thread today ŌĆö it imports `javafx.concurrent.Task`, `PieChart`, and `ProjectDiskUsage` (verified) and streams files via `java.nio.file.Files`/`Stream`. The absorbed version must *preserve* that off-thread computation behind the ┬¦5.8 "ŌŚĘ recomputingŌĆ”" affordance, marshalling only the result back with `Platform.runLater`.
- **┬¦5.7 ŌĆö reset reaches the per-category tier.** Story 306 shipped per-setting reset, story 307 per-group; this story adds per-category reset (┬¦5.7 tier 3: "Reset Recording to defaults" / "Reset Backups to defaults" in the pane's Ōŗ» menu), leaving only the guarded restore-all for story 309.

Per ┬¦7 Stage 4, the standalone entry points for both dialogs become deep links into the surface rather than separate windows.

## Goals

- **Recording category absorbs `MetronomeSettingsDialog` (┬¦3.3).** A Recording category in the story-306 rail renders the metronome routing as generic setting rows (┬¦5.4) under the ┬¦3.3 groups ŌĆö Metronome (enable, count-in), Click routing (side-output channel, gain, direct-output enable), and Cue mix (main-mix sum, cue bus). Every control currently in `MetronomeSettingsDialog` (the `ClickOutput` routing it edits ŌĆö side-output channel, gain, main-mix sum, hardware-enable, and the story-135 cue selector) has a row driven by a story-305 descriptor; no metronome setting remains reachable only through a separate window.
- **Backups category absorbs `BackupSettingsDialog`, pie included (┬¦3.3, ┬¦5.8).** A Backups category renders the retention policy as rows (Keep last N autosaves, Keep for N days, Min free space N GB ŌĆö ┬¦5.8) under a Retention group, and the disk-usage pie (`PieChart` over `ProjectDiskUsage`) as a *group-level visual* in a Disk usage group (┬¦5.8), not a modal. The pie reads the same `BackupRetentionService`/`ProjectDiskUsage` data source it does today (story 244) and renders inside the category.
- **Disk-usage pie recomputes off the FX thread (┬¦2.7, ┬¦6.6, ┬¦5.8).** Preserve the existing `Task`-based off-thread computation; surface it as the "ŌŚĘ recomputingŌĆ”" progress affordance in the Disk usage group header and apply the result via `Platform.runLater`. The settings surface stays responsive while disk usage is scanned. Prefer a virtual thread / `StructuredTaskScope` so closing the surface cancels an in-flight scan cleanly (`java-26.agent.md` Project Loom) ŌĆö an upgrade from the current `javafx.concurrent.Task` if it simplifies cancellation, but the *off-thread* guarantee is the invariant, not the mechanism.
- **Standalone entry points become deep links (┬¦7 Stage 4).** The menu/toolbar/notification entry points that open `MetronomeSettingsDialog` and `BackupSettingsDialog` today are repointed to open the settings surface focused on the Recording / Backups category, respectively ŌĆö not the standalone modal. Per `feedback_test_only_callers_are_not_live_usage.md`, delete each dialog class if its only remaining callers are its own tests (migrating still-valid assertions onto the row/catalogue path); keep and repoint it if a live entry still needs it.
- **Per-category reset (┬¦5.7 tier 3).** Each category's pane Ōŗ» menu gains "Reset Recording to defaults" / "Reset Backups to defaults", resetting every setting in that category to its descriptor default (building on the per-setting `Ōå║` of story 306 and the per-group reset of story 307). This is ┬¦5.7 tier 3; the guarded restore-all (tier 4) is story 309.
- **Tokens, never literals (┬¦2.6).** The Recording/Backups rows, the retention sliders, and the pie's legend use semantic tokens (`text-muted` for descriptions, chart-series/elevation tokens for the pie), not inline hex/font-weight (rejection #2). The category titles are plain labels, not accent fills (┬¦5.3).
- **Project-vs-app scope is correct in metadata (┬¦3.2).** Metronome routing that belongs to the open project is tagged `THIS_PROJECT`; retention policy is `APPLICATION` (the book's ┬¦3.2 examples). The on-screen scope chip is story 309; here the *metadata* must be right so 309 can surface it.
- Tests:
  - `MetronomeDialogAbsorbedTest` (new): the settings surface exposes a Recording category with rows for every `MetronomeSettingsDialog` control; the standalone `MetronomeSettingsDialog` is no longer opened as a nested/standalone modal from its former entry points (the ┬¦2.1 guard).
  - `BackupDialogAbsorbedTest` (new): the settings surface exposes a Backups category with retention rows and the disk-usage pie rendered inline (not as a modal); the former entry point deep-links into the Backups category.
  - `DiskUsagePieOffFxThreadTest` (new): the disk-usage computation runs on a non-FX thread and applies via `Platform.runLater`; a slow scan does not block category construction (substitute a fake slow scanner). Capture+rethrow any assertion inside an FX runnable (`feedback_javafx_headless_test_pitfalls.md`); extract the pure usage-aggregation math and test it with no toolkit.
  - `RecordingAndBackupsCategoryResetTest` (new): "Reset Recording to defaults" restores all Recording settings without touching other categories; likewise for Backups.
  - `AbsorbedScopeMetadataTest` (new): metronome routing settings are `THIS_PROJECT` scope and retention settings are `APPLICATION` scope in the catalogue (so story 309's chip is correct).
  - `DeepLinkFocusesCategoryTest` (new): invoking the former metronome entry opens the settings surface with the Recording category selected; invoking the former backup entry selects the Backups category.

## Non-Goals

- **No change to backup retention semantics or the disk-usage data source.** The pie reads the same `BackupRetentionService`/`ProjectDiskUsage` it does today (story 244); this story re-homes the view, not the computation's meaning. Retention policy values keep their existing `Preferences` keys and `SettingsModel`/`BackupRetentionPolicy` representation.
- **No change to metronome audio behaviour.** Click routing / cue-bus *audio* (stories 016/056/084, the `ClickOutput`/`CueBus` types `MetronomeSettingsDialog` edits) is unchanged; this story re-homes the *settings UI* for it. The descriptor's validator delegates to the existing setter.
- **No scope chip on screen.** Scope metadata is corrected here (`THIS_PROJECT` / `APPLICATION`); the ┬¦5.3 scope tag is surfaced uniformly in story 309.
- **No import/export and no restore-all.** Story 309 (┬¦5.9, ┬¦5.7 tier 4). Per-category reset (tier 3) is the ceiling here.
- **No new `THIS_PROJECT` persistence machinery.** If a metronome setting is genuinely per-project, it continues to persist exactly where it does today; the `THIS_PROJECT` tag records the reach (┬¦3.2) without inventing a new project-file field in this story. Net-new per-project persistence, if any control needs it, is flagged as adjacent (`feedback_scope_fix_defer_tangential.md`), not built here.
- **No embedded-panel promotion.** The surface stays modal (┬¦4.B deferred to ┬¦7 Stage 5 "optionally").
